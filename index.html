Welcome — Let's Keep It Simple

You don't need experience. Just read, try, and learn at your pace.

Have you ever walked into an office and thought "where is everything?" or "how does anything get done here?" — you're not alone. A lot of people feel that way on Day 1.

This lesson teaches you a simple system called O.R.D.E.R. Each letter stands for one skill. Learn all five, and you'll know how to run an office properly.

What Does an Office Admin Actually Do?

If you're new to office work, here's a plain English answer: an office administrator keeps everything running. Think of them as the person who makes sure the ship doesn't sink.

  • 📁Makes sure documents are saved and easy to find
  • 🖨️Makes sure the printer has paper and the pens aren't missing
  • 📅Helps the team stay organized with schedules and to-do lists
  • 📞Handles calls, emails, and messages professionally
  • 🧹Keeps the shared spaces neat and functional
  • 🤝Supports other team members when they need help

Module 1 · Organize

Organizing the Office

Making sure everything has a place — and everyone knows where that place is.

1 of 5
🤔

Think about this first: Imagine you need a stapler urgently. Do you know exactly where it is? Could a new colleague find it without asking anyone? That's what "organized" means.

What is Organization?

Organization just means giving everything a home — a specific place where it always lives. When things have a home, people stop wasting time searching.

Good organization has three parts:

🏢

The Physical Space

Desks, shelves, drawers, cabinets — is everything neatly placed and labeled?

💻

The Computer

Folders, files, emails — are documents saved with clear names in logical folders?

⚙️

How Work Flows

Do people know what to do, in what order, and who does what?

The 5S Method — Explained Simply

5S is a famous cleaning-and-organizing method. The name comes from five Japanese words. Here's what each step means in everyday language:

#The StepWhat It MeansExample in the Office
1️⃣SortThrow out what you don't needThrow away old papers, remove broken pens
2️⃣Set in OrderGive everything a fixed spotLabel the stationery drawer, add signs to shelves
3️⃣ShineKeep things clean and tidyWipe desks every Friday, archive old files monthly
4️⃣StandardizeWrite down the rules so everyone follows themMake a rule: "Always return items to their labeled spot"
5️⃣SustainMake it a habit, not a one-time thingDo a 5-minute tidy check every morning
Naming Files on a Computer

One of the biggest problems in offices is files that are saved with confusing names like "final version 3 REVISED NEW.docx". Nobody knows what that is!

❌ Bad File Names

document1.docx  |  final.pdf  |  copy of report (2).xlsx  |  ASDFGH.docx

✅ Good File Names

2024-06-15_Finance_BudgetReport.pdf  |  2024-06_HR_LeaveApplication_JuanDela.docx

A good file name tells you: when it was made, what department it belongs to, and what it is. Think of it like a label on a box.

Office Zones — Where Things Happen

A well-organized office is divided into zones. Each zone has a purpose. Think of it like a house — you cook in the kitchen, sleep in the bedroom. The same idea applies to an office.

  • 🤫Quiet Zone — for focused work, reading, or tasks that need concentration
  • 💬Team Zone — open area for group discussions, quick check-ins, collaboration
  • 📦Resource Zone — where the printer, supplies, and filing cabinets live
  • 🚪Meeting Zone — a separate room or area for formal discussions and calls

Module 2 · Record

Managing Files & Documents

Think of this as giving the office a reliable memory — so nothing important gets lost.

2 of 5
📖

Real talk: Imagine your boss asks for last month's supplier invoice — right now. Could you find it in 2 minutes? If not, this module will fix that.

Why Keeping Good Records Matters

Records are the office's memory. They prove what happened, what was agreed, and what was spent. Without proper records, the office makes the same mistakes over and over.

Good records also protect you. If there's ever a dispute or an audit, having proper documentation is your best defense.

Types of Documents You'll Handle
📧

Correspondence

Emails, letters, memos — communication records between people and organizations.

💰

Financial Docs

Invoices, receipts, purchase orders — anything involving money.

👤

Personnel Files

Employee contracts, leave records, performance reviews.

📜

Legal Documents

Contracts, permits, licenses — official agreements with legal weight.

How to Build a Simple Filing System

A filing system is just a set of folders — physical or digital — organized in a way that makes sense. Here are three popular ways to organize files:

MethodHow It WorksBest For
🔤 AlphabeticalSort A–Z by name or companyClient files, supplier records
📅 By DateSort by year → month → dayInvoices, letters, meeting minutes
🏷️ By TopicGroup by subject or departmentPolicies, projects, HR documents
💡

Most offices use a mix: For example, topic folders (HR, Finance, Operations) organized by date inside each folder.

How Long Do You Keep Documents?

Not everything needs to be kept forever. But throwing things away too soon can get the office in trouble. Here's a simple guide:

Document TypeKeep ForThen What?
General letters & emails2 yearsDelete or shred
Financial records7 yearsShred securely
Employee records7 years after they leaveShred securely
Legal contractsDuration + 7 yearsAsk a supervisor first
Permanent policiesForeverMove to long-term archive
3 Golden Rules for Documents
  • 1️⃣One master version only. Never have two copies of the "final" document floating around.
  • 2️⃣Sensitive files stay locked. Employee records and financial docs go in locked cabinets or password-protected folders.
  • 3️⃣Shred, don't bin. Documents with names, money amounts, or personal info must be shredded — not just thrown in the trash.

Module 3 · Delegate

Sharing Work & Managing Time

Making sure tasks go to the right person — and get done on time.

3 of 5
😅

Sound familiar? One person is drowning in tasks while another is scrolling their phone. That's a delegation problem — and it's fixable.

What is Delegation?

Delegation means giving a task to someone else with clear instructions. It's NOT dumping work on people. Done right, it helps everyone — the manager gets breathing room, and the team member gets to grow.

First — Sort Your Tasks by Priority

Before you can delegate, you need to know which tasks to keep and which to hand off. Use this simple 4-box system:

⚡ Urgent🕰️ Not Urgent
⭐ Important 🔴 DO NOW
Crisis, deadline, emergency		 🟡 PLAN IT
Training, improvements, planning
💤 Not Important 🔵 DELEGATE IT
Interruptions, some meetings		 ⚫ DROP IT
Busy work, time-wasters
🎯

The goal: Spend most of your time in the "Important, Not Urgent" box. That's where real progress happens — planning ahead, not putting out fires.

How to Delegate Properly

When you give someone a task, don't just say "can you handle this?" That's how things fall through the cracks. Instead, give them five things:

  • 🎯What exactly needs to be done — describe the task clearly
  • ✅What "done" looks like — how will you know it's finished correctly?
  • ⏰When it's due — give a specific date and time, not "soon"
  • 🔑What they're allowed to do — can they make decisions, or must they check with you?
  • 📞When to update you — agree on a check-in point so you're not left guessing
❌ Vague Delegation

"Hey, can you sort out the supplies situation?"

✅ Clear Delegation

"Please check the stationery cupboard, list what's running low, and email me the list by Thursday 3pm. You can order directly for items under ₱500 — anything higher, send me the quote first."

Why People Struggle to Delegate
Common ExcuseThe Truth
"It's faster if I do it myself"True once. But teaching someone takes 10 minutes and saves hours later.
"No one will do it as well as me"Maybe at first. But they'll improve — and you'll stop burning out.
"I don't know who to give it to"Make a simple list of your team members and what they're good at.

Module 4 · Equip

Equipment & Supplies

Making sure the office has what it needs — before it runs out.

4 of 5
😬

Worst timing: The printer runs out of toner 10 minutes before an important presentation. This module teaches you how to prevent that.

Two Things to Manage
🖥️

Equipment

Big items that last a long time — computers, printers, projectors, shredders, furniture.

📦

Supplies

Items that get used up — paper, pens, toner, folders, sticky notes, cleaning products.

Track Your Equipment — The Asset List

For equipment, you need an asset list — a simple record of every major item in the office. Think of it as a logbook for your equipment.

For each item, record:

  • 🏷️What it is (brand, model)
  • 📅When it was bought and how much it cost
  • 📍Where it is right now
  • 👤Who is responsible for it
  • 🛠️When it was last serviced or repaired
  • ⚠️Whether the warranty is still valid
📝 Example Asset List Entry

HP LaserJet Pro M404n · Purchased Jan 2022 · ₱18,500 · Located: Admin Room · Assigned to: Office Admin · Last serviced: March 2024 · Warranty expired: Jan 2024

Manage Supplies — The Min/Max System

The simplest way to never run out of supplies is the minimum-maximum system:

TermWhat It MeansExample (Bond Paper)
MaximumThe most you'll ever store10 reams
MinimumThe least you'll allow before reordering2 reams
Reorder PointWhen you place the orderWhen stock hits 3 reams
Order QuantityHow much you order each time7 reams (back to max)
💡

Simple trick: Put a sticky note inside your supply cabinet at the "reorder level." When the stack reaches that note, it's time to order.

Prevent Breakdowns Before They Happen

Machines break down less often when you take care of them regularly. This is called preventive maintenance — like servicing a car before it breaks down on the highway.

EquipmentWhat to DoHow Often
🖨️ Printer / CopierClean, check toner, clear paper jams, test feedMonthly
🖨️ Printer / CopierProfessional service from the supplierOnce a year
💻 ComputersUpdate software, clear old files, restartEvery 3 months
🗑️ ShredderOil the blades, empty the binWeekly
🔌 All EquipmentCheck cables, look for damage, test if workingMonthly

Module 5 · Reflect

The Capstone: Put It All Together

Use everything you've learned to fix a real office problem — from scratch.

5 of 5 🎉
🧠

What is "Reflect"? It means stopping to ask: "What did we do? Did it work? What can we do better next time?" The best office workers don't just do tasks — they improve continuously.

The Improvement Loop

Good office workers follow a simple habit loop to keep improving:

📝

1. Plan

Decide what you're going to do and how you'll do it.

🚀

2. Do

Carry out the plan as best you can.

👀

3. Check

Did it work? What went well? What didn't?

🔄

4. Improve

Make adjustments. Then plan again.
